Section I

This photo goes all the way back to 1911 with Woodrow Wilson addressing a crowd from the balcony of the Lafayette Hotel. Cape May was and still is famous for hosting public figures.

Section II

The beauty and relaxing feel surrounding Cape May goes back over 100 years. One of the reasons Cape May remains a popular East Coast destination is its beautifully restored Victorian-era hotels and B&Bs.

Section III

Nothing in Cape May is as famous as Congress Hall. It began life in 1816 as a simple boarding house for summer visitors to one of America's earliest seaside resorts. Today it is the heart of tourism in this sprawling beach town.

Section IV

Erected in 1857, the Cape May lighthouse has been a staple of the towns rich history. The lighthouse had 2 predecessors that only stood for roughly 80 years, they modeled the current lighthouse after the lighthouse built in Barneget for it sturdy construction and expected longevity.

Section V

At the heart of its popularity above all else is its beaches. Cape May County beaches in the southern end of the Jersey shore are known for their wide, fine, powdery sand, and are considered to be among the best beaches on the Atlantic coast.
